# 01. Overview

The Penny Hockey project was a tabletop game board that combined woodworking, laser cutting, 3D printing, soldering, and Arduino programming. The board features a NASA logo laser engraved into clear plexiglass, surrounded by a wooden frame supported by dado cuts and assembled with miter joints.

# 02. Build Details

The goals for the penny were made by drilling large corner holes, cutting the openings with a jigsaw, and inserting custom 3D-printed parts so the penny could slide smoothly. Additional printed parts were used for the feet and for holding the electronic components in a compact form factor.

- **Laser engraved** plexiglass playing surface
- **Wooden frame** with dado cuts and miter joints
- **3D-printed** goal inserts, feet, and electronics holder
- **Assembly** via snap fitting, gluing, nailing, and screw-based methods

---

# 03. Electronics

LEDs and electronic components were soldered and wired together using solid-core wire so the wiring could hold its shape. The project also pushed the Arduino code beyond simple single-action behavior, requiring control of LEDs, LCDs, a piezo, and pushbuttons in a way that felt simultaneous to the player.

The project improved 3D modeling, soldering, wiring, and embedded programming skills, especially around packaging several physical and electronic systems into one small playable object.s
