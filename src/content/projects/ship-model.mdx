# 01. USS Missouri BB-63

<div class="not-prose rounded-lg overflow-hidden border border-slate-200 dark:border-slate-800 bg-slate-100 dark:bg-slate-950">
  <img src="/assets/ship-model/Missouri.jpg" alt="USS Missouri BB-63 scale model" class="w-full h-auto object-cover" />
</div>

Built from Tamiya's 1/700 Missouri kit, this model includes aftermarket metal photo-etched parts, gun barrels, and a wooden deck.

### Build Process
- Hull and deck bonded with plastic cement, then masked and brush painted with Measure 22 camouflage.
- Superstructure assembled, detailed with photo-etched parts, and acrylic brush painted in haze gray.
- Wooden deck painted blue and attached, with fine details such as cannons, anti-aircraft weaponry, stairs, and railings added afterward.
- Weathering applied with oil paint and acrylic wash, followed by rigging, flags, and a glossy wet-deck effect.
- Ocean base shaped from foam, air-dry clay, epoxy, paint, and cotton wave crests.

The full build process took around seven months, working a few hours a day for a few days per week.

<div class="not-prose rounded-lg overflow-hidden border border-slate-200 dark:border-slate-800 bg-slate-100 dark:bg-slate-950">
  <img src="/assets/ship-model/Missouri_His.jpg" alt="Historical USS Missouri reference" class="w-full h-auto object-cover" />
</div>

USS Missouri BB-63 was an Iowa-class battleship commissioned in 1944. She served in World War II, the Korean War, and Operation Desert Storm, and is best known as the site of Japan's formal surrender on September 2, 1945.

# 02. USS Atlanta CL-51

<div class="not-prose rounded-lg overflow-hidden border border-slate-200 dark:border-slate-800 bg-slate-100 dark:bg-slate-950">
  <img src="/assets/ship-model/Atlanta.jpg" alt="USS Atlanta CL-51 scale model" class="w-full h-auto object-cover" />
</div>

Built from the Very Fire 1/350 USS Atlanta Deluxe kit, this model includes plastic hull parts, sprues, photo-etched parts, and brass gun barrels.

### Build Process
- Hull, bridge, funnels, and superstructure sections bonded with plastic cement.
- Photo-etched details such as porthole rims and railings attached with CA glue.
- Late 1942 camouflage brush painted by hand, including hull, deck, and superstructure patterns.
- Fine parts, masts, weathering, rigging, clear coats, and flags added for final detail.

The build took about six months, working a few days per week for a couple of hours at a time.

<div class="not-prose rounded-lg overflow-hidden border border-slate-200 dark:border-slate-800 bg-slate-100 dark:bg-slate-950">
  <img src="/assets/ship-model/Atlanta_his.jpg" alt="Historical USS Atlanta reference" class="w-full h-auto object-cover" />
</div>

USS Atlanta CL-51 was the lead ship of the Atlanta-class light cruisers. Designed as an anti-aircraft cruiser, she served in the Pacific and played a major role in the Battle of Guadalcanal before being scuttled after heavy damage on November 13, 1942.
