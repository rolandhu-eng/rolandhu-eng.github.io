Project

Schlieren Imaging

Visualizing Fluid Flow

Optics CAD Arduino

Overview

Most fascinating airflow phenomena are completely invisible to the naked eye. However, with the aid of a simple DIY setup, the invisible becomes visible. Schlieren imaging allows the normally invisible density gradients in air to become visible. On a hot summer day, you might have seen distortions when looking close to the horizon. This is due to hot air heated by the ground becoming less dense, which refracts light differently than the surrounding cooler air, causing images to be distorted. Schlieren imaging takes advantage of this changing index of refraction caused by changes in density, enhancing the effect enough that even hot air due to your body temperature can be seen.

Highlights

  • Built a compact optical setup to visualize density gradients
  • Tuned alignment and lighting for higher contrast
  • Explored structured mounts to keep optics stable
